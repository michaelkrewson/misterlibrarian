Two annunciations and two songs — Gabriel to Zechariah in the temple and to Mary in Nazareth, the Visitation, the birth of John, the Magnificat and the Benedictus.
c. 6–5 BC, 'in the days of Herod the king' (Herod the Great died in 4 BC) — the six months before the birth of Jesus. Luke's preface (1:1–4) is the one place a Gospel opens with a dated, first-person dedication.

Luke 1 — The Mister Translation

1
Ἐπειδήπερ πολλοὶ ἐπεχείρησαν ἀνατάξασθαι διήγησιν περὶ τῶν πεπληροφορημένων ἐν ἡμῖν πραγμάτων,
Since many have undertaken to compile an account of the things that have been fully accomplished among us,note
Puesto que muchos han emprendido poner en orden un relato de las cosas que entre nosotros se han cumplido plenamente,
2
καθὼς παρέδοσαν ἡμῖν οἱ ἀπʼ ἀρχῆς αὐτόπται καὶ ὑπηρέται γενόμενοι τοῦ λόγου,
just as those who from the beginning were eyewitnesses and became servants of the word handed them down to us,note
tal como nos las transmitieron los que desde el principio fueron testigos oculares y llegaron a ser servidores de la palabra,
3
ἔδοξε κἀμοὶ παρηκολουθηκότι ἄνωθεν πᾶσιν ἀκριβῶς καθεξῆς σοι γράψαι, κράτιστε Θεόφιλε,
it seemed good to me too, having followed all things closely from the start, to write them out for you in orderly sequence, most excellent Theophilus,note
me pareció bien también a mí, habiéndolo investigado todo con cuidado desde el origen, escribírtelas por orden, excelentísimo Teófilo,
4
ἵνα ἐπιγνῷς περὶ ὧν κατηχήθης λόγων τὴν ἀσφάλειαν.
so that you may know the certainty of the things about which you were instructed.note
para que conozcas la certeza de las cosas en que fuiste instruido.
5
Ἐγένετο ἐν ταῖς ἡμέραις Ἡρῴδου βασιλέως τῆς Ἰουδαίας ἱερεύς τις ὀνόματι Ζαχαρίας ἐξ ἐφημερίας Ἀβιά, καὶ γυνὴ αὐτῷ ἐκ τῶν θυγατέρων Ἀαρών, καὶ τὸ ὄνομα αὐτῆς Ἐλισάβετ.
In the days of Herod, king of Judea, there was a certain priest named Zechariah, of the division of Abijah, and he had a wife from the daughters of Aaron, and her name was Elizabeth.note
En los días de Herodes, rey de Judea, hubo cierto sacerdote llamado Zacarías, de la clase de Abías, y tenía una esposa de las hijas de Aarón, y su nombre era Elisabet.
6
ἦσαν δὲ δίκαιοι ἀμφότεροι ἐναντίον τοῦ θεοῦ, πορευόμενοι ἐν πάσαις ταῖς ἐντολαῖς καὶ δικαιώμασιν τοῦ κυρίου ἄμεμπτοι.
And they were both righteous before God, walking blamelessly in all the commandments and requirements of the Lord.note
Y ambos eran justos delante de Dios, andando irreprensibles en todos los mandamientos y preceptos del Señor.
7
καὶ οὐκ ἦν αὐτοῖς τέκνον, καθότι ἦν ἡ Ἐλισάβετ στεῖρα, καὶ ἀμφότεροι προβεβηκότες ἐν ταῖς ἡμέραις αὐτῶν ἦσαν.
And they had no child, because Elizabeth was barren, and both were advanced in their days.note
Y no tenían hijo, porque Elisabet era estéril, y ambos eran de edad avanzada en sus días.
8
Ἐγένετο δὲ ἐν τῷ ἱερατεύειν αὐτὸν ἐν τῇ τάξει τῆς ἐφημερίας αὐτοῦ ἔναντι τοῦ θεοῦ
Now it happened, while he was serving as priest before God in the order of his division,note
Y sucedió que, ejerciendo él el sacerdocio delante de Dios en el orden de su clase,
9
κατὰ τὸ ἔθος τῆς ἱερατείας ἔλαχε τοῦ θυμιᾶσαι εἰσελθὼν εἰς τὸν ναὸν τοῦ κυρίου,
according to the custom of the priesthood, the lot fell to him to burn incense, entering into the sanctuary of the Lord.note
conforme a la costumbre del sacerdocio, le tocó en suerte quemar el incienso, entrando en el santuario del Señor.
10
καὶ πᾶν τὸ πλῆθος ἦν τοῦ λαοῦ προσευχόμενον ἔξω τῇ ὥρᾳ τοῦ θυμιάματος·
And the whole multitude of the people was praying outside at the hour of the incense.note
Y toda la multitud del pueblo estaba orando fuera, a la hora del incienso.
11
ὤφθη δὲ αὐτῷ ἄγγελος κυρίου ἑστὼς ἐκ δεξιῶν τοῦ θυσιαστηρίου τοῦ θυμιάματος.
And an angel of the Lord appeared to him, standing at the right of the altar of incense.note
Y se le apareció un ángel del Señor, de pie a la derecha del altar del incienso.
12
καὶ ἐταράχθη Ζαχαρίας ἰδών, καὶ φόβος ἐπέπεσεν ἐπʼ αὐτόν.
And Zechariah was troubled at the sight, and fear fell upon him.note
Y Zacarías se turbó al verlo, y el temor cayó sobre él.
13
εἶπεν δὲ πρὸς αὐτὸν ὁ ἄγγελος· Μὴ φοβοῦ, Ζαχαρία, διότι εἰσηκούσθη ἡ δέησίς σου, καὶ ἡ γυνή σου Ἐλισάβετ γεννήσει υἱόν σοι, καὶ καλέσεις τὸ ὄνομα αὐτοῦ Ἰωάννην·
But the angel said to him, "Do not be afraid, Zechariah, because your petition has been heard, and your wife Elizabeth will bear you a son, and you shall call his name John.note
Pero el ángel le dijo: «No temas, Zacarías, porque tu súplica ha sido oída, y tu esposa Elisabet te dará a luz un hijo, y llamarás su nombre Juan.
14
καὶ ἔσται χαρά σοι καὶ ἀγαλλίασις, καὶ πολλοὶ ἐπὶ τῇ γενέσει αὐτοῦ χαρήσονται·
And you will have joy and gladness, and many will rejoice at his birth,note
Y tendrás gozo y alegría, y muchos se regocijarán por su nacimiento,
15
ἔσται γὰρ μέγας ἐνώπιον τοῦ κυρίου, καὶ οἶνον καὶ σίκερα οὐ μὴ πίῃ, καὶ πνεύματος ἁγίου πλησθήσεται ἔτι ἐκ κοιλίας μητρὸς αὐτοῦ,
for he will be great before the Lord. He must never drink wine or strong drink, and he will be filled with the Holy Spirit already from his mother's womb,note
porque será grande delante del Señor. No beberá nunca vino ni bebida fuerte, y será lleno del Espíritu Santo ya desde el vientre de su madre,
16
καὶ πολλοὺς τῶν υἱῶν Ἰσραὴλ ἐπιστρέψει ἐπὶ κύριον τὸν θεὸν αὐτῶν·
and he will turn many of the sons of Israel back to the Lord their God.note
y hará volver a muchos de los hijos de Israel al Señor su Dios.
17
καὶ αὐτὸς προελεύσεται ἐνώπιον αὐτοῦ ἐν πνεύματι καὶ δυνάμει Ἠλίου, ἐπιστρέψαι καρδίας πατέρων ἐπὶ τέκνα καὶ ἀπειθεῖς ἐν φρονήσει δικαίων, ἑτοιμάσαι κυρίῳ λαὸν κατεσκευασμένον.
And he will go on ahead before him in the spirit and power of Elijah, to turn the hearts of fathers to children and the disobedient to the wisdom of the righteous, to make ready for the Lord a people prepared."note
Y él irá delante de él en el espíritu y el poder de Elías, para hacer volver los corazones de los padres a los hijos, y a los desobedientes a la sabiduría de los justos, para preparar al Señor un pueblo dispuesto».
18
καὶ εἶπεν Ζαχαρίας πρὸς τὸν ἄγγελον· Κατὰ τί γνώσομαι τοῦτο; ἐγὼ γάρ εἰμι πρεσβύτης καὶ ἡ γυνή μου προβεβηκυῖα ἐν ταῖς ἡμέραις αὐτῆς.
And Zechariah said to the angel, "By what shall I know this? For I am an old man, and my wife is advanced in her days."note
Y Zacarías dijo al ángel: «¿En qué conoceré esto? Porque yo soy anciano, y mi esposa es de edad avanzada en sus días».
19
καὶ ἀποκριθεὶς ὁ ἄγγελος εἶπεν αὐτῷ· Ἐγώ εἰμι Γαβριὴλ ὁ παρεστηκὼς ἐνώπιον τοῦ θεοῦ, καὶ ἀπεστάλην λαλῆσαι πρὸς σὲ καὶ εὐαγγελίσασθαί σοι ταῦτα·
And the angel answered and said to him, "I am Gabriel, who stands before God, and I was sent to speak to you and to bring you this good news.note
Y respondiendo el ángel le dijo: «Yo soy Gabriel, que estoy delante de Dios, y fui enviado a hablarte y a traerte esta buena noticia.
20
καὶ ἰδοὺ ἔσῃ σιωπῶν καὶ μὴ δυνάμενος λαλῆσαι ἄχρι ἧς ἡμέρας γένηται ταῦτα, ἀνθʼ ὧν οὐκ ἐπίστευσας τοῖς λόγοις μου, οἵτινες πληρωθήσονται εἰς τὸν καιρὸν αὐτῶν.
And behold, you will be silent and unable to speak until the day these things happen, because you did not believe my words, which will be fulfilled in their time."note
Y mira, quedarás callado y sin poder hablar hasta el día en que sucedan estas cosas, porque no creíste mis palabras, que se cumplirán a su tiempo».
21
Καὶ ἦν ὁ λαὸς προσδοκῶν τὸν Ζαχαρίαν, καὶ ἐθαύμαζον ἐν τῷ χρονίζειν ἐν τῷ ναῷ αὐτόν.
And the people were waiting for Zechariah, and they wondered at his lingering in the sanctuary.note
Y el pueblo estaba esperando a Zacarías, y se extrañaban de que se demorara en el santuario.
22
ἐξελθὼν δὲ οὐκ ἐδύνατο λαλῆσαι αὐτοῖς, καὶ ἐπέγνωσαν ὅτι ὀπτασίαν ἑώρακεν ἐν τῷ ναῷ· καὶ αὐτὸς ἦν διανεύων αὐτοῖς, καὶ διέμενεν κωφός.
But when he came out he could not speak to them, and they realised that he had seen a vision in the sanctuary; and he kept making signs to them and remained mute.note
Pero al salir no podía hablarles, y comprendieron que había visto una visión en el santuario; y él les hacía señas, y permanecía mudo.
23
καὶ ἐγένετο ὡς ἐπλήσθησαν αἱ ἡμέραι τῆς λειτουργίας αὐτοῦ, ἀπῆλθεν εἰς τὸν οἶκον αὐτοῦ.
And it happened, when the days of his service were completed, that he went away to his house.note
Y sucedió que, cumplidos los días de su servicio, se fue a su casa.
24
Μετὰ δὲ ταύτας τὰς ἡμέρας συνέλαβεν Ἐλισάβετ ἡ γυνὴ αὐτοῦ· καὶ περιέκρυβεν ἑαυτὴν μῆνας πέντε, λέγουσα
Now after these days Elizabeth his wife conceived, and she kept herself hidden five months, saying,note
Y después de estos días concibió Elisabet su esposa, y se mantuvo escondida cinco meses, diciendo:
25
ὅτι Οὕτως μοι πεποίηκεν κύριος ἐν ἡμέραις αἷς ἐπεῖδεν ἀφελεῖν ὄνειδός μου ἐν ἀνθρώποις.
"Thus the Lord has done for me in the days when he looked on me, to take away my disgrace among people."note
«Así ha hecho conmigo el Señor en los días en que se dignó quitar mi oprobio entre los hombres».
26
Ἐν δὲ τῷ μηνὶ τῷ ἕκτῳ ἀπεστάλη ὁ ἄγγελος Γαβριὴλ ἀπὸ τοῦ θεοῦ εἰς πόλιν τῆς Γαλιλαίας ᾗ ὄνομα Ναζαρὲθ
Now in the sixth month the angel Gabriel was sent from God to a city of Galilee named Nazareth,note
Y en el mes sexto el ángel Gabriel fue enviado de parte de Dios a una ciudad de Galilea llamada Nazaret,
27
πρὸς παρθένον ἐμνηστευμένην ἀνδρὶ ᾧ ὄνομα Ἰωσὴφ ἐξ οἴκου Δαυίδ, καὶ τὸ ὄνομα τῆς παρθένου Μαριάμ.
to a virgin betrothed to a man named Joseph, of the house of David, and the virgin's name was Mary.note
a una virgen desposada con un hombre llamado José, de la casa de David; y el nombre de la virgen era María.
28
καὶ εἰσελθὼν πρὸς αὐτὴν εἶπεν· Χαῖρε, κεχαριτωμένη, ὁ κύριος μετὰ σοῦ
And coming in to her he said, "Rejoice, favoured one — the Lord is with you."note
Y entrando el ángel adonde ella estaba, dijo: «Alégrate, favorecida — el Señor está contigo».
29
ἡ δὲ ἐπὶ τῷ λόγῳ διεταράχθη καὶ διελογίζετο ποταπὸς εἴη ὁ ἀσπασμὸς οὗτος.
But she was greatly troubled at the word, and she kept wondering what kind of greeting this might be.note
Pero ella se turbó mucho por la palabra, y se preguntaba qué clase de saludo sería este.
30
καὶ εἶπεν ὁ ἄγγελος αὐτῇ· Μὴ φοβοῦ, Μαριάμ, εὗρες γὰρ χάριν παρὰ τῷ θεῷ·
And the angel said to her, "Do not be afraid, Mary, for you have found favour with God.note
Y el ángel le dijo: «No temas, María, porque has hallado favor delante de Dios.
31
καὶ ἰδοὺ συλλήμψῃ ἐν γαστρὶ καὶ τέξῃ υἱόν, καὶ καλέσεις τὸ ὄνομα αὐτοῦ Ἰησοῦν.
And behold, you will conceive in your womb and bear a son, and you shall call his name Jesus.note
Y mira, concebirás en tu vientre y darás a luz un hijo, y llamarás su nombre Jesús.
32
οὗτος ἔσται μέγας καὶ υἱὸς Ὑψίστου κληθήσεται, καὶ δώσει αὐτῷ κύριος ὁ θεὸς τὸν θρόνον Δαυὶδ τοῦ πατρὸς αὐτοῦ,
He will be great and will be called Son of the Most High, and the Lord God will give him the throne of David his father,note
Este será grande y será llamado Hijo del Altísimo, y el Señor Dios le dará el trono de David su padre,
33
καὶ βασιλεύσει ἐπὶ τὸν οἶκον Ἰακὼβ εἰς τοὺς αἰῶνας, καὶ τῆς βασιλείας αὐτοῦ οὐκ ἔσται τέλος.
and he will reign over the house of Jacob forever, and of his kingdom there will be no end."note
y reinará sobre la casa de Jacob para siempre, y de su reino no habrá fin».
34
εἶπεν δὲ Μαριὰμ πρὸς τὸν ἄγγελον· Πῶς ἔσται τοῦτο, ἐπεὶ ἄνδρα οὐ γινώσκω;
And Mary said to the angel, "How will this be, since I do not know a man?"note
Y María dijo al ángel: «¿Cómo será esto, puesto que no conozco varón?»
35
καὶ ἀποκριθεὶς ὁ ἄγγελος εἶπεν αὐτῇ· Πνεῦμα ἅγιον ἐπελεύσεται ἐπὶ σέ, καὶ δύναμις Ὑψίστου ἐπισκιάσει σοι· διὸ καὶ τὸ γεννώμενον ἅγιον κληθήσεται, υἱὸς θεοῦ·
And the angel answered and said to her, "The Holy Spirit will come upon you, and the power of the Most High will overshadow you; therefore the one to be born will be called holy, the Son of God.note
Y respondiendo el ángel le dijo: «El Espíritu Santo vendrá sobre ti, y el poder del Altísimo te cubrirá con su sombra; por lo cual también el que ha de nacer será llamado santo, Hijo de Dios.
36
καὶ ἰδοὺ Ἐλισάβετ ἡ συγγενίς σου καὶ αὐτὴ συνείληφεν υἱὸν ἐν γήρει αὐτῆς, καὶ οὗτος μὴν ἕκτος ἐστὶν αὐτῇ τῇ καλουμένῃ στείρᾳ·
And behold, Elizabeth your relative — she too has conceived a son in her old age, and this is the sixth month for her who was called barren;note
Y mira, Elisabet tu parienta, ella también ha concebido un hijo en su vejez, y este es el sexto mes para la que llamaban estéril;
37
ὅτι οὐκ ἀδυνατήσει παρὰ τοῦ θεοῦ πᾶν ῥῆμα.
for no word will be impossible with God."note
porque ninguna palabra será imposible para Dios».
38
εἶπεν δὲ Μαριάμ· Ἰδοὺ ἡ δούλη κυρίου· γένοιτό μοι κατὰ τὸ ῥῆμά σου. καὶ ἀπῆλθεν ἀπʼ αὐτῆς ὁ ἄγγελος.
And Mary said, "Behold, the bondslave of the Lord; may it happen to me according to your word." And the angel went away from her.note
Y María dijo: «Aquí está la esclava del Señor; que me suceda conforme a tu palabra». Y el ángel se fue de ella.
39
Ἀναστᾶσα δὲ Μαριὰμ ἐν ταῖς ἡμέραις ταύταις ἐπορεύθη εἰς τὴν ὀρεινὴν μετὰ σπουδῆς εἰς πόλιν Ἰούδα,
And Mary arose in those days and went into the hill country with haste, to a city of Judah,note
Y en aquellos días se levantó María y fue de prisa a la región montañosa, a una ciudad de Judá,
40
καὶ εἰσῆλθεν εἰς τὸν οἶκον Ζαχαρίου καὶ ἠσπάσατο τὴν Ἐλισάβετ.
and she entered the house of Zechariah and greeted Elizabeth.note
y entró en la casa de Zacarías y saludó a Elisabet.
41
καὶ ἐγένετο ὡς ἤκουσεν τὸν ἀσπασμὸν τῆς Μαρίας ἡ Ἐλισάβετ, ἐσκίρτησεν τὸ βρέφος ἐν τῇ κοιλίᾳ αὐτῆς, καὶ ἐπλήσθη πνεύματος ἁγίου ἡ Ἐλισάβετ,
And it happened, when Elizabeth heard Mary's greeting, the baby leaped in her womb, and Elizabeth was filled with the Holy Spirit,note
Y sucedió que, cuando Elisabet oyó el saludo de María, la criatura saltó en su vientre, y Elisabet fue llena del Espíritu Santo,
42
καὶ ἀνεφώνησεν κραυγῇ μεγάλῃ καὶ εἶπεν· Εὐλογημένη σὺ ἐν γυναιξίν, καὶ εὐλογημένος ὁ καρπὸς τῆς κοιλίας σου.
and she cried out with a loud cry and said, "Blessed are you among women, and blessed is the fruit of your womb.note
y clamó a gran voz y dijo: «Bendita tú entre las mujeres, y bendito el fruto de tu vientre.
43
καὶ πόθεν μοι τοῦτο ἵνα ἔλθῃ ἡ μήτηρ τοῦ κυρίου μου πρὸς ἐμέ
And how has this come to me, that the mother of my Lord should come to me?note
¿Y de dónde me viene esto, que la madre de mi Señor venga a mí?
44
ἰδοὺ γὰρ ὡς ἐγένετο ἡ φωνὴ τοῦ ἀσπασμοῦ σου εἰς τὰ ὦτά μου, ἐσκίρτησεν ἐν ἀγαλλιάσει τὸ βρέφος ἐν τῇ κοιλίᾳ μου.
For behold, as the sound of your greeting came into my ears, the baby leaped for gladness in my womb.note
Porque mira, en cuanto llegó la voz de tu saludo a mis oídos, la criatura saltó de alegría en mi vientre.
45
καὶ μακαρία ἡ πιστεύσασα ὅτι ἔσται τελείωσις τοῖς λελαλημένοις αὐτῇ παρὰ κυρίου.
And happy is she who believed that there would be a fulfilment of the things spoken to her from the Lord."note
Y feliz la que creyó, porque habrá cumplimiento de las cosas que le fueron dichas de parte del Señor».
46
Καὶ εἶπεν Μαριάμ· Μεγαλύνει ἡ ψυχή μου τὸν κύριον,
And Mary said:
My soul magnifies the Lord,note
Y María dijo:
Mi alma engrandece al Señor,
47
καὶ ἠγαλλίασεν τὸ πνεῦμά μου ἐπὶ τῷ θεῷ τῷ σωτῆρί μου·
and my spirit has rejoiced in God my Saviour,note
y mi espíritu se ha regocijado en Dios mi Salvador,
48
ὅτι ἐπέβλεψεν ἐπὶ τὴν ταπείνωσιν τῆς δούλης αὐτοῦ, ἰδοὺ γὰρ ἀπὸ τοῦ νῦν μακαριοῦσίν με πᾶσαι αἱ γενεαί·
because he has looked on the low estate of his bondslave.
For behold, from now on all generations will call me happy,note
porque miró la humilde condición de su esclava.
Porque mira, desde ahora todas las generaciones me llamarán feliz,
49
ὅτι ἐποίησέν μοι μεγάλα ὁ δυνατός, καὶ ἅγιον τὸ ὄνομα αὐτοῦ,
because the Mighty One has done great things for me —
and holy is his name,note
porque el Poderoso ha hecho grandes cosas por mí —
y santo es su nombre,
50
καὶ τὸ ἔλεος αὐτοῦ εἰς γενεὰς καὶ γενεὰς τοῖς φοβουμένοις αὐτόν.
and his mercy is for generations and generations
on those who fear him.note
y su misericordia es de generación en generación
para los que le temen.
51
Ἐποίησεν κράτος ἐν βραχίονι αὐτοῦ, διεσκόρπισεν ὑπερηφάνους διανοίᾳ καρδίας αὐτῶν·
He has done a mighty deed with his arm;
he has scattered the proud in the thoughts of their hearts.note
Hizo proeza con su brazo;
dispersó a los soberbios en el pensamiento de sus corazones.
52

He has brought down rulers from thrones
and lifted up the lowly;note
Derribó a los poderosos de sus tronos
y levantó a los humildes;
53

the hungry he has filled with good things,
and the rich he has sent away empty.note
a los hambrientos colmó de bienes
y a los ricos despidió vacíos.
54
ἀντελάβετο Ἰσραὴλ παιδὸς αὐτοῦ, μνησθῆναι ἐλέους,
He has taken hold of Israel his servant,
to remember mercy —note
Amparó a Israel su siervo,
para acordarse de la misericordia —
55
καθὼς ἐλάλησεν πρὸς τοὺς πατέρας ἡμῶν, τῷ Ἀβραὰμ καὶ τῷ σπέρματι αὐτοῦ εἰς τὸν αἰῶνα.
just as he spoke to our fathers —
to Abraham and to his seed forever.note
tal como habló a nuestros padres —
a Abraham y a su descendencia para siempre.
56
Ἔμεινεν δὲ Μαριὰμ σὺν αὐτῇ ὡς μῆνας τρεῖς, καὶ ὑπέστρεψεν εἰς τὸν οἶκον αὐτῆς.
And Mary stayed with her about three months, and returned to her house.note
Y María se quedó con ella como tres meses, y volvió a su casa.
57
Τῇ δὲ Ἐλισάβετ ἐπλήσθη ὁ χρόνος τοῦ τεκεῖν αὐτήν, καὶ ἐγέννησεν υἱόν.
Now the time was fulfilled for Elizabeth to give birth, and she bore a son.note
Y a Elisabet se le cumplió el tiempo de dar a luz, y dio a luz un hijo.
58
καὶ ἤκουσαν οἱ περίοικοι καὶ οἱ συγγενεῖς αὐτῆς ὅτι ἐμεγάλυνεν κύριος τὸ ἔλεος αὐτοῦ μετʼ αὐτῆς, καὶ συνέχαιρον αὐτῇ.
And her neighbours and relatives heard that the Lord had magnified his mercy toward her, and they rejoiced with her.note

Translator’s Notes

Verses 1–4 · a doctor writes a Greek preface

The only Gospel that opens like a book. These four verses are a single, balanced, classical Greek sentence — the most polished Greek in the New Testament — the kind of periodic preface a Greek historian or physician would put at the head of a serious work. Then Luke drops it and shifts, from verse 5 on, into the plain Septuagint-flavoured Greek of the Hebrew scriptures. He can write like Thucydides and chooses to write like the Old Testament: the announcement is deliberate. This is the one place in the four Gospels where the author steps forward and speaks as I.

"Most excellent Theophilus." The name means "friend of God" or "loved by God" — possibly a real patron, possibly every reader who loves God. Kratistos ("most excellent") is a form of address for a person of rank; Luke uses it again for the governors Felix and Festus in Acts. He addresses the same Theophilus at the start of Acts (1:1), which is the seam that makes Luke and Acts one two-volume work by one author.

"That you may know the certainty." Asphaleia — firmness, security, the opposite of stumbling. Luke says outright why he is writing: not to move the emotions but to give solid ground under things the reader has already been catechised in (katecheo, the root of "catechism"). He is a careful witness who "followed all things closely from the start," and early tradition names him a physician and a travelling companion of Paul. KJV "the certainty of those things"; the NWT "the certainty."

Verses 5–7 · a barren couple, on purpose

"In the days of Herod." Luke anchors everything in datable history — Herod the Great, the priestly courses, later a Roman census, a named emperor and governor. The story is placed inside the calendar of the world, not in "once upon a time."

The division of Abijah. David split the priests into twenty-four rotating courses (1 Chronicles 24); Abijah's was the eighth. Zechariah is an ordinary rural priest, married to a daughter of Aaron — priestly on both sides. Both are called "righteous" and "blameless," which makes their childlessness a grief, not a punishment.

⚠ Barren and advanced in years. The words are chosen to make the reader hear the old stories: Sarah and Abraham, Rebekah, Rachel, Hannah, the Shunammite. Israel's whole history begins with wombs God opens against the odds. Luke's Gospel opens the same way — and then, one chapter later, will out-do all of them with a womb that needs no man at all.

Verses 8–10 · the lot of a lifetime

The lot fell to him. With so many priests, burning the incense in the sanctuary was a once-in-a-lifetime honour assigned by lot; most priests never drew it. Zechariah is at the single greatest moment of his priestly life — alone in the holy place, at the hour of incense, the smoke standing for the prayers of the people — when the answer to his own long-abandoned prayer walks up beside the altar.

The people praying outside. The whole crowd is at prayer in the courts while the priest is within; the gospel's first scene is a congregation waiting outside a closed door for a man who is taking too long.

Verses 11–17 · a second Elijah

Never wine or strong drink. John is marked out from the womb like a lifelong Nazirite (Numbers 6) — the vow of Samson and Samuel — and "filled with the Holy Spirit from his mother's womb," which verse 41 will show literally when he leaps at Mary's voice.

"In the spirit and power of Elijah." The angel closes by quoting Malachi — the last verse of the last prophet: "he will turn the hearts of fathers to children" (Malachi 4:5–6). Malachi promised Elijah before the great day of the Lord; Gabriel says the promise is now walking toward its fulfilment. John is not Elijah returned in body, but Elijah's role and clothing and fire, sent to "make ready a people prepared." The four-hundred-year silence since Malachi ends with a sentence of Malachi.

Verses 18–20 · the priest who could not believe

⚠ "By what shall I know this?" Watch this line, because Luke means you to hold it against Mary's question in verse 34. Zechariah, the righteous priest who has spent his life at the altar, asks for a proof and is silenced for his unbelief. Mary, an unmarried girl in a nowhere town, asks only how, and is answered and blessed. The learned man of religion doubts; the young woman believes. Luke's Gospel keeps making that reversal, and it starts here, in the temple, with a mute priest.

"I am Gabriel, who stands before God." The same angel who came to Daniel with the timetable of the end (Daniel 8–9) now comes to time the beginning. The silence is both a punishment and a sign: Zechariah asked for a sign, and became one.

Verses 21–25 · the taking away of a disgrace

"To take away my disgrace." Oneidos — reproach, shame. Childlessness was read as a reproach a woman carried in public; Elizabeth's five months of hiding are the quiet of someone scarcely daring to believe. Her words echo Rachel's at the birth of Joseph, "God has taken away my reproach" (Genesis 30:23) — another closed womb opened, another child who would save a family and a nation.

Verses 26–33 · favoured one

⚠ "Rejoice, favoured one" — the words behind the "Hail Mary." Kecharitomene is a perfect passive participle of charitoo, from charis, grace/favour: "one who has been graced, favoured." The old Latin rendered it gratia plena, "full of grace," which the "Hail Mary" carries — but the Greek says she is one who has received favour, not one who is a reservoir of it to dispense. The verse names her the object of God's kindness, and verse 30 spells it plainly: "you have found favour with God," the same thing said of Noah. This translation keeps the plain sense and flags the famous crux rather than smuggling a doctrine into a participle. KJV "highly favoured"; the ESV and NWT "favored one."

The throne of David, and no end. Gabriel folds the covenant to David (2 Samuel 7) into the message: an heir on David's throne over the house of Jacob, a kingdom "with no end." It is the oldest royal promise in Israel, delivered to a girl in Nazareth.

Verses 34–38 · the overshadowing, and the fiat

"Since I do not know a man." A plain statement of virginity — parthenos, virgin, from verse 27. Her question is not Zechariah's demand for proof; it is a real "how," and it is answered.

"Will overshadow you." Episkiazo — the verb the Greek Old Testament uses for the cloud of glory that "overshadowed" the tabernacle so that the presence of God filled it (Exodus 40:35). The same shadow that once filled the tent now falls on a woman; she becomes the place where God dwells.

⚠ "No word will be impossible with God." Almost exactly the sentence the visitor spoke to Sarah at the oaks of Mamre — "Is anything too hard for the LORD?" (Genesis 18:14) — where the same word, rhema, can mean both "word" and "thing." Luke ties the newest birth to the oldest.

"The bondslave of the Lord." Doule — a female slave, the strongest word for it. Mary answers not with resignation but with a soldier's assent: "may it happen to me according to your word." The whole scene turns on it, and where Zechariah was struck dumb, Mary's yes becomes, twelve verses later, a song.

Verses 39–45 · the leap in the womb

The baby leaped. Skirtao — to leap, skip; the Greek Old Testament uses it for the children struggling in Rebekah's womb (Genesis 25:22). Unborn John recognises unborn Jesus and jumps; the forerunner is already going ahead, before either is born. Elizabeth, filled with the Spirit, is the first human being in this Gospel to confess who Mary carries: "the mother of my Lord."

"Happy is she who believed." Makaria — the beatitude word, "happy, blessed." Elizabeth blesses Mary not for her womb but for her faith — and the line lands like a verdict on the whole chapter, because it is exactly what Zechariah failed to do. Belief, not biology, is what she is called happy for.

Verses 46–55 · the Magnificat

Mary's song. Named the Magnificat from its first Latin word, "magnifies." It is built almost entirely out of the Old Testament, and above all out of Hannah's song at the birth of Samuel (1 Samuel 2:1–10) — another poor woman whose God-given child would turn a nation. Mary sings in the past tense, as though the reversal is already done.

"The low estate of his bondslave." Tapeinosis — lowliness, humiliation. God's regard falls on the lowly, and the song then names the reversal in six hammer-strokes: the proud scattered, rulers pulled off thrones, the lowly lifted, the hungry filled, the rich sent away empty. It is the most politically dangerous poem in the Gospels, and it is sung by a teenage girl. "All generations will call me happy" (makarizo) — the same root as verse 45.

"To Abraham and to his seed forever." The song ends where the promises began: mercy remembered, the oath to Abraham kept. What God is doing is not a new departure but the ancient covenant coming due.

Verses 56–58 · a mercy magnified

"The Lord had magnified his mercy toward her." The verb is the same root as the Magnificat's opening — Mary sang that her soul magnifies the Lord, and now the neighbours say the Lord has magnified his mercy for Elizabeth. The joy the angel promised in verse 14 arrives on schedule.

Verses 59–66 · the naming, and the loosened tongue

"No — he shall be called John." On the eighth day, at the circumcision, the neighbours assume the boy takes his father's name; both parents refuse, insisting on the name the angel gave — John, "the Lord is gracious." Zechariah, still mute, writes it on a tablet, and the moment his obedience matches Elizabeth's, his tongue is freed. The sign given for his doubt is lifted the instant he acts on faith; his first freed word is a blessing of God. The whole hill country senses that a hand is on this child.

Verses 67–79 · the Benedictus — the priest sings back

Zechariah's prophecy. Named the Benedictus, "blessed." The priest who was silenced for unbelief is now filled with the Spirit and opens with praise; his song, like Mary's, is woven from covenant promises — the "horn of salvation" in the house of David (a horn is an OT image of strength, Psalm 132:17), the oath sworn to Abraham, deliverance to serve God "without fear."

⚠ "The dawn from on high." Anatole is a deliberate double word: it means both the rising of the sun and, in the Greek Old Testament, the messianic "Branch" — the shoot promised to sprout from David (Jeremiah 23:5; Zechariah 3:8; 6:12). Zechariah's ears would hear both: a light dawning and a Branch springing. It shines "on those sitting in darkness and the shadow of death," Isaiah's own words (Isaiah 9:2). KJV "the dayspring from on high"; the ESV "the sunrise"; the NWT "a daybreak." Each catches half of a word that carries both.

The child addressed. Zechariah turns and speaks to his eight-day-old son (verse 76): "you will go on ahead before the Lord to prepare his ways." The Elijah role Gabriel named in verse 17 is now spoken over the boy by his own father.

Verse 80 · and then, silence again

"He was in the wilderness until the day of his public showing." With one sentence Luke hides John for thirty years. The forerunner grows up in the desert — the same wilderness where prophets were made — and the curtain falls on him until it rises again in chapter 3, at the Jordan, "the word of God came to John." The song is sung; now comes the long waiting.

Patterns worth carrying forward

Where this translation keeps the exact / distinct words: “most excellent” for kratistos and “certainty” for asphaleia, keeping the historian’s register of the preface; “favoured one” for kecharitomene, one who has received favour, not a container of it; “bondslave” for doule, the strongest word, kept for both Mary’s yes and her song; “overshadow” for episkiazo, the tabernacle-cloud verb; “happy” for makaria/makarizo, the beatitude word, kept the same in Elizabeth’s blessing and Mary’s song; and “the dawn from on high” for anatole, following the rising-light sense while the note keeps the “Branch” that rides underneath it.

Echoes paid. A barren, aged, righteous couple set against Sarah, Hannah, and every opened womb in Israel; Malachi’s last promise of Elijah (Malachi 4:5–6) quoted over John; “no word will be impossible with God” carried straight from Sarah at Mamre (Genesis 18:14); the Magnificat built on Hannah’s song (1 Samuel 2); the Benedictus on the oath to Abraham and the “Branch” of Jeremiah and Zechariah; and Isaiah’s “those sitting in darkness” (Isaiah 9:2) lit at the end.

Echoes planted. The doubting priest and the believing girl, a reversal the whole Gospel will keep making; the Holy Spirit, poured on Elizabeth, Mary, and Zechariah before a single miracle — Luke’s signature; the Magnificat’s reversal (the lowly lifted, the rich sent away empty), waiting to be paid in the Beatitudes and the woes of chapter 6; the leaping forerunner, going ahead of Jesus before birth as he will at the Jordan; and John hidden “in the wilderness,” the curtain that rises again in chapter 3.

Next in this book: Luke 2 — a decree from Caesar Augustus, a census, a birth in Bethlehem with no room in the inn, shepherds and a sky full of angels, the child presented in the temple to old Simeon and the prophetess Anna, and the one glimpse of Jesus as a boy, left behind in his Father’s house.
