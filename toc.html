Genesis 1The seven days — day one, the vault, and the image of God.Genesis 2The sabbath, the divine name arrives, and “side,” not “rib.”Genesis 3The serpent, the fall, and the naked/crafty pun that spans the chapter break.Genesis 4Cain and Abel, the first murder, and “am I my brother’s keeper?”Genesis 5Ten generations, one drumbeat — and the one man who never dies.Genesis 6The sons of God, the Nephilim, Jehovah’s regret, and the ark.Genesis 7The flood: creation run in reverse, and “Jehovah shut him in.”Genesis 8God remembers Noah — the raven, the dove, and the first altar.Genesis 9Meat and blood, the first law, and the bow hung in the clouds.Genesis 10The Table of Nations: the whole known world, drawn as one family tree.Genesis 11Babel and babble — and the quiet road to Ur.Genesis 12Lekh lekha: the call of Abram, and Egypt as the Exodus in miniature.Genesis 13Abram and Lot part ways — the land too small for both, and the Hebrew word for “separate” that decides everything.Genesis 14The war of the kings, Abram rescues Lot, and Melchizedek — bread, wine, the first tithe, and the first “Hebrew.”Genesis 15The covenant of the pieces — the stars, “counted to him as righteousness,” and the God who walks between the halves alone.Genesis 16Hagar and Ishmael — the Bible's first angel, the runaway slave-girl, and the God who sees.Genesis 17The covenant in the flesh — El Shaddai, Abram becomes Abraham, Sarai becomes Sarah, and a face-down laugh names Isaac.Genesis 18Three visitors at Mamre, Sarah's laugh — and Abraham arguing the Judge of all the earth down to ten.Genesis 19Sodom's last night — the two angels at the gate, sulfur and fire at sunrise, a pillar of salt, and the cave above Zoar.Genesis 20The sister-ruse replayed at Gerar — Abimelech's dream, the Bible's first 'prophet,' and the pagan king with the integrity.John 1The Word made flesh — the Prologue and its “was God / a god,” the Lamb of God, and the first disciples.John 2Water into wine at Cana — the beginning of the signs — and the temple cleared at the first Passover: “destroy this temple.”Revelation 1The unveiling begins — Patmos, the Lord's day, one like a son of man among the lampstands, and the Alpha and the Omega.Revelation 2The letters begin — Ephesus's lost first love, Smyrna's crown, Satan's throne at Pergamum, and Jezebel of Thyatira.Daniel 1Babylon takes its first captives — four renamed youths, a ten-day test of vegetables and water, and 'ten hands better' than the magicians.Daniel 11The angel's scroll of wars — Persia, Alexander, the kings of south and north, the abomination that desolates — and the seam where history becomes hope.Daniel 12The sleepers in the ground of dust wake — everlasting life named for the first time, the sealed book, two numbers nobody has decoded, and a lot promised at the end of the days.Matthew 5The Sermon on the Mount opens in red letters — eight Happy-sayings, salt and light, not one iota, and six rounds of 'You have heard… but I say': anger, lust, oaths, the other cheek, love for enemies.Matthew 6The Sermon's middle chapter — alms, prayer, and fasting in secret; the Lord's Prayer and its famous missing doxology; treasure, the undivided eye, Mammon — and the birds and the lilies.Matthew 7The Sermon's finale — the splinter and the beam, ask-seek-knock, the Golden Rule signed, the narrow gate, wolves and fruit, 'I never knew you' — and the house on the rock.Jeremiah 20Pashhur and the stocks — the prophet renames his jailer Terror-All-Around, names Babylon at last, confesses the fire shut up in his bones — and curses the day he was born.Jeremiah 21The final siege — Zedekiah's delegation asks for a miracle and hears the bleakest answer in the book: the Exodus formula aimed inward, the way of life through the enemy camp, and fire for the cedar forest.Genesis 21Isaac is born and the laughter lands — then Hagar's second desert scene ('God heard the boy, there where he is'), seven ewe-lambs, the well of the oath, and the Everlasting God at a tamarisk.
